Revenue Tracking using Power BI

A reputable company offering a diverse range of products was facing difficulties in maximizing their sales potential due to a lack of insight into key revenue factors. As a business analyst, my role was to develop a revenue tracking dashboard that featured: total revenue, orders, and product quantities; a monthly revenue trend (preferably broken down by shipping route); the top 5 revenue-generating products and employees; and interactive filters for order date, supplier, and customer to provide a more customized analysis for management.

Dashboard for Tailored Analysis

A well-established clothing company offering a diverse range of apparel, struggled with a fragmented understanding of their sales performance and product trends. They depended on scattered data and guesswork for important decisions regarding product lines, marketing campaigns, and inventory management. This approach resulted in missed opportunities, inefficient inventory management, and a narrow marketing focus. To address these issues, we needed to create both a sales dashboard and a product dashboard. The dashboards featured metrics for total orders, revenue, goods sold, and customer count. They included monthly revenue trends, a map of revenue by country with detailed tooltips, top customers by revenue and order volume, and filters for year, gender, product category, and continent. This analysis was carried out utilising Power BI.
